About

The Fake News Detection System is a final-year project developed with the aim of addressing the growing concern of misinformation in the digital age. Under the esteemed guidance of Prof. Arpit Chaudhari, this project has been designed to accurately classify news articles as real or fake using Natural Language Processing (NLP) and deep learning techniques. By leveraging a pre-trained BERT model along with advanced text vectorization methods, our system effectively analyzes the linguistic patterns and credibility of news content to ensure high accuracy and efficiency in detection. This initiative was undertaken by a dedicated team consisting of: Team Leader: Prathamesh Raut Team Members: Parth Deshmukh, Bhavesh Gujar, Pavankumar Sonune The system is built with a user-friendly interface that enables journalists, researchers, and the general public to verify news authenticity effortlessly. Through extensive data preprocessing, training on large datasets, and optimizing deep learning models, this project aspires to contribute to the fight against fake news by promoting responsible information dissemination and enhancing digital media reliability.
